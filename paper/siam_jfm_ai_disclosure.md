# SIAM AI/LLM Use Disclosure Draft

SIAM's AI policy states that authors must be human beings, that all co-authors
remain responsible for integrity and originality, and that AI use beyond
spelling/style polishing must be documented in the acknowledgements of the
submitted work.

Draft disclosure for human-author review:

> Portions of the manuscript drafting, code scaffolding, experiment automation,
> documentation, and validation scripts were prepared with assistance from
> OpenAI Codex, a GPT-5 based coding assistant, on May 16, 2026. The tool was
> used interactively to inspect and edit source files, run experiments, prepare
> reproducibility documentation, and summarize validation evidence. The human
> human authors are responsible for all mathematical claims, proofs, citations, code,
> data processing, numerical experiments, and final text included in the
> submitted manuscript.

Before submission, the corresponding author should either insert an approved
version of this text into the manuscript acknowledgements or replace it with a
more precise disclosure matching the final human workflow.
